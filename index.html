Research

My research broadly include Scientific Foundation Models, Time Series Modeling, Application of Multi-modal models for scientific tasks, LLMs for time-series and Physics-guided ML.
Currently, I am building a foundation model for lake ecosystems, LakeFM. Some of the challenges I am trying to address are pretraining strategies for training large models, learning on irregular and missing multivariate data, cross-frequency learning and knowledge transfer from simulation to real-world data.
In addition, I am also exploring model-agnostic techniques for robust Time Series modeling (MissTSM); Time Series Reasoning for enhancing interpretability modeling performance with exogenous variables (work ongoing around a large hierarchial medical Time-series dataset); Knowledge-distillation from LLMs for interpretable time-series modeling and prototype discovery
My research around Multi-modal models (especially, Vision Language Models) has been broadly applied work - empirical evaluation of reasoning abilities of VLMs on scientific species data (VLM4Bio). Currently, I am collaborating on a research involving scientific equation discovery using VLMs and LLMs.
I am also collaborating on research involving generative modeling approaches, particularly diffusion models, to solve Parital Differential Equations (PDEs) through physics-guided learning.
