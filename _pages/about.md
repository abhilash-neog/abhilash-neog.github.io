Hi :wave: Welcome to my personal space !

I am a CS PhD student at Virginia Tech, currently working as a Graduate Research Assistant at the Knowledge-guided Machine Learning Lab under the guidance of [Prof. Anuj Karpatne](https://people.cs.vt.edu/karpatne/). Prior to that, I completed my Bachelor's from BITS Pilani, India in Computer Science and later worked as a Data scientist at Oracle for a year and a half before finally joining VT.

Since my undergrad, I have had a major interest in Machine Learning, and I have been fortunate enough to have explored diverse areas ranging from computer vision to natural language processing and have applied ML to variety of domains ranging from ecology to the financial sector. Presently, my research interests include Knowledge-guided Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing and Time-series modeling. Overall, I am interested in AI for Science, especially in the domains of cognitive science and climate science.

My current work is primarily in Time-series modeling and Foundation models. Recently, I have started working on building a Foundation (or universal) model for lake data forecasting. Some of the challenges I am trying to address are data scarcity, cross-frequency learning, missing data, data types (modeled, observed) and data distributions. Moreover, I am also interested in the active field of LLM (Large Language Model) trustworthiness. Some of my recent work involve domain-specific evaluation of the reasoning abilities of pre-trained LLMs and VLMs (Vision-Language Model) under zero-shot setting; studying hallucination/factual error propagation in Large Language Models.

If you share similar interests or just want to talk, please feel free to drop me an email!

Apart from academia, I love spending time outdoors, especially cycling in the beautiful trails of Blacksburg and going for hikes. Moreover, I am passionate about soccer and table tennis, and recently have started learning the violin too !
